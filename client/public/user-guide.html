1 Getting Started — Your First Free Check

Run a compliance check in under 60 seconds. No account or credit card needed.

1

Go to taptrao.com

Click "Free Pre-Shipment Check" on the homepage, or click "New Check" in the sidebar if you're already inside the app.

2

Select your commodity

Open the "What are you shipping?" dropdown. Choose from 190+ commodities, each with their HS code shown. You can scroll or type to search.

3

Select the origin country

Under "Where are the goods coming from?", select the African country of origin. All major African trading nations are listed with their flag emoji.

4

Select the destination

Under "Where are the goods going?", choose the import destination: EU, UK, USA, Switzerland, Türkiye, UAE, or Canada.

5

Click "Check compliance risk"

The system analyses regulatory requirements, duty rates, known hazards, document obligations, and trade restrictions for your specific corridor. Results appear in about 2 seconds.

Your first check is always free — no card required. After that, each check uses 1 TapTrao Shield credit.

2 Understanding Your Compliance Results

What you'll see after running a compliance check.

A

TwinLog Score (0–100)

A readiness score that tells you how complex this trade is. The score is based on four factors:

FactorWhat It MeasuresMax Penalty
Regulatory RequirementsNumber of active regulatory overlays (SPS, EUDR, CBAM, etc.)30
Product ControlsKnown hazards for this commodity (e.g. cadmium, mycotoxins)30
Document VolumeTotal number of documents you'll need20
Trade RestrictionsSanctions, embargoes, or special authorisations20

Score interpretation:

70–100: LOW RISK   40–69: MODERATE RISK   0–39: HIGH RISK

B

EUDR Warning (if applicable)

If your commodity triggers the EU Deforestation Regulation, you'll see a yellow banner with a link to "Prepare EUDR documents". This takes you to a form where you can add geolocation data for your production plots.

C

Next Actions

A prioritised list of all documents and actions required, showing:

  • Document name and description
  • Who is responsible (You, Supplier, or Broker)
  • When it's needed (Before loading, Before arrival, Post-arrival)
  • Current status (Pending, Ready, Risk Accepted)
D

Trade Corridor Details

Three cards showing the Commodity (with HS code), Origin (with customs authority and trade bloc), and Destination (with VAT rate and tariff system).

E

Regulatory Triggers

Red badges showing which regulations apply: SPS EUDR CBAM CSDDD and others. Grey badges show regulations that do NOT apply to this trade.

F

Watch This Corridor

Click "Watch this corridor" to receive notifications when regulations change for this specific trade route. You can watch up to 3 corridors for free.

G

Workflow Steps

At the top of the results, you'll see a 4-step progress indicator: Lookup → LC Check → TwinLog Trail → Archive. These guide you through the full compliance workflow for this shipment.

5 Requesting Supplier Documents

Ask your supplier to upload documents directly — no email attachments.

1

Open your trade and click "Request Documents"

From the trade detail page, click the button to request documents from your supplier. TapTrao creates a secure upload link automatically.

2

Share the upload link

Copy the generated link and send it to your supplier via email or WhatsApp. The link is unique to this trade and doesn't require the supplier to create an account.

3

Supplier uploads their documents

Your supplier sees a simple upload form listing the required documents (Commercial Invoice, Certificate of Origin, Packing List, etc.). They upload each file and submit.

4

Review in your Supplier Inbox

Go to "Suppliers" in the sidebar to see all received documents. Each upload shows the document type, filename, and upload timestamp.

Tip: You can also upload documents yourself (as the buyer) from the trade detail page. This is useful for documents you prepare on your side, like a revised Commercial Invoice.

6 Verifying & Flagging Documents

Review supplier documents and build your audit trail.

1

Open a document for review

From the trade detail page, find the uploaded documents section. Each document has a "Verify" button and a "Flag" button.

2

Verify correct documents

Click Verify to confirm the document is correct. This adds a "Document Verified" event to the trade's audit trail.

3

Flag problematic documents

Click Flag to mark a document as having an issue. You'll be asked to enter a finding (e.g. "Country of origin shows Ghana but trade corridor is Cote d'Ivoire"). This creates a "Document Flagged" event in the audit trail.

Every action is recorded. Verifications, flags, status changes, and document uploads are all logged in the trade's event timeline with cryptographic hashes for tamper detection.

7 LC Document Checker

Cross-check your supplier documents against the Letter of Credit terms using UCP 600 rules.

1

Enter LC Terms (Step 1)

Fill in the key terms from your Letter of Credit: beneficiary name, applicant name, goods description, HS code, quantity, amount, currency, incoterms, ports, and shipping dates.

2

Enter Supplier Documents (Step 2)

For each supplier document, enter the key fields. The system checks: Commercial Invoice, Bill of Lading, Certificate of Origin, Packing List, Insurance Certificate, and more.

3

Review & Submit (Step 3)

Review your entries and click submit. The engine applies UCP 600 and ISBP 745 rules to find discrepancies.

4

View Results (Step 4)

Results show a verdict with three possible outcomes:

COMPLIANT — No discrepancies found

COMPLIANT WITH NOTES — Minor issues to be aware of

DISCREPANCIES FOUND — Issues that could cause bank rejection

Each discrepancy shows the UCP 600 article, which document is affected, and a suggested correction.

Cost: LC Document Check uses 1 Shield credit. If linked to a compliance lookup (from the trade workflow), it's included free. Re-checks after corrections are $9.99 each, with 3 free re-checks per case.

8 Downloading Your TwinLog Trail

Generate an audit-ready PDF with full compliance evidence.

1

Set up your Company Profile first

Go to Settings → Company Profile and fill in your company name, registered address, country, VAT number, and EORI number. This information appears on every TwinLog Trail.

2

Navigate to step 3 "TwinLog Trail"

From the trade detail page (after running a compliance check), click step 3 in the workflow indicator, or scroll down to the TwinLog section.

3

Click "Download TwinLog Trail"

The PDF is generated instantly and downloaded to your computer. It contains 8 pages:

PageContents
1. CoverCommodity, corridor, company details, reference number
2. TwinLog ScoreReadiness score breakdown with factors and penalties
3. Importer RequirementsAll documents and actions required of the importing party
4. Supplier RequirementsDocuments the supplier must provide, with issuing authorities
5. Supplier DocumentsTable of all uploaded documents with verification status
6. Regulatory OverlaysActive regulations and known hazards for the commodity
7. Event TimelineComplete audit trail with timestamps, events, and hash chain
8. Audit RecordSHA-256 integrity hash, reference, timestamps, chain verification
Each TwinLog Trail has a unique reference (e.g. TT-2026-50FF2F) and an SHA-256 integrity hash. Anyone can verify the record at taptrao.com/verify/TT-2026-XXXXXX.

9 Setting Up Your Company Profile

Required for TwinLog Trail PDF generation.

Go to Settings in the sidebar, then fill in:

FieldDescriptionRequired
Company NameAs it appears on your Certificate of IncorporationYes
Registered Office AddressFull address including postcode/ZIP. Must match regulatory documents.Yes
Country of RegistrationSelect from dropdownYes
VAT NumberUsed for EU/UK trade recordsNo
EORI NumberEconomic Operators Registration and Identification. Required by UK/EU customs to track every shipment.No (but strongly recommended)
Why EORI matters: Your EORI number is what customs authorities use to identify you on every import declaration. A compliance record without an EORI cannot be linked to your customs activity. UK traders can register at gov.uk; EU traders via their national customs authority.

12 Demurrage Calculator

Estimate port demurrage costs to understand the financial impact of delays.

Go to "Demurrage Calc" in the sidebar under Tools. Enter:

  • Base daily rate (from your shipping contract)
  • Number of chargeable days (days past the free period)

The calculator uses an escalating tier model: days 1-7 at base rate, days 8-14 at 1.5x, and days 15+ at 2x. This helps you quantify the cost of document delays.

? Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to create an account?

No. TapTrao uses session-based identity. Your data is tied to your browser session automatically. No registration, no passwords.

What happens if I clear my browser cookies?

Your session data will no longer be accessible. We recommend downloading your TwinLog Trail PDFs before clearing cookies so you always have your compliance records.

Is the compliance data up to date?

TapTrao's regulatory database is updated regularly. However, regulations change frequently. We recommend running a fresh compliance check before each shipment. Your TwinLog Trail reflects the regulatory state at the time it was generated.

Can my supplier see my compliance results?

No. The supplier upload link only shows the document upload form. Suppliers cannot see your compliance results, TwinLog Score, or any other data.

Is TapTrao legal advice?

No. TapTrao is a compliance information tool. It does not constitute legal or trade advice. The importing party remains responsible for ensuring all regulatory requirements are met. Always consult with your customs broker or legal advisor for specific situations.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept all major credit and debit cards through Stripe (Visa, Mastercard, Amex). All payments are processed securely.

Can I get a refund?

Credits are non-refundable once a compliance check has been run. Unused credits do not expire.
