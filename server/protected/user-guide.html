1 Getting Started

What TapTrao does, what you see on your first visit, and how to navigate the application.

What is TapTrao?

TapTrao is a trade compliance platform built for commodity traders who ship goods from African origin countries to international destinations. It replaces scattered spreadsheets, manual regulatory lookups, and guesswork with a structured, audit-ready compliance workflow.

With TapTrao you can:

  • Check tariffs, regulatory requirements, and compliance obligations for any commodity corridor
  • Cross-check Letter of Credit terms against supplier documents using UCP 600 rules
  • Generate TwinLog Trail PDFs — audit-ready evidence records with SHA-256 integrity hashes
  • Track documents from suppliers via secure, token-gated upload links
  • Watch trade corridors for regulatory changes with real-time alerts
  • Estimate demurrage costs when port delays occur

Your first visit

When you open TapTrao for the first time, you land on the homepage. No account creation is required. TapTrao assigns you a session automatically via a browser cookie, and all your data is tied to that session. There is no username, password, or email to remember.

The TapTrao landing page with the green hero section and navigation bar

From the landing page, click "Free Pre-Shipment Check" to jump straight into your first compliance check. You can also scroll down to learn about features, pricing, and the LC Document Checker before diving in.

Sidebar navigation

Once inside the application (after clicking "Free Pre-Shipment Check" or "Dashboard"), a sidebar appears on the left side of the screen. This sidebar is your primary navigation and is organised into three groups:

SectionItemsPurpose
MenuDashboard, New Check, My TradesCore workflow actions
ComplianceLC Checker, Templates, Alerts, Demurrage CalcSpecialist compliance tools
AccountSupplier Inbox, Settings, Buy CreditsAccount management and supplier communication

The sidebar also shows your recent trade history at the bottom, with trade corridor tags (e.g. "Cocoa CI → EU") so you can quickly jump back to a previous check.

No account needed. TapTrao uses a browser cookie to remember your session. Your compliance checks, documents, and credits are all preserved as long as you don't clear your browser cookies. We recommend downloading TwinLog Trail PDFs regularly as a permanent backup.
Tip: On mobile devices (screen width below 768px), the sidebar collapses into a bottom tab bar. Tap the icons to navigate between sections.

2 Running Your First Compliance Check

Step-by-step: select your commodity, origin, and destination, then check compliance risk.

A compliance check analyses the regulatory requirements, duty rates, known hazards, document obligations, and trade restrictions for a specific trade corridor (commodity + origin country + destination market). Here is exactly how to run one:

1

Navigate to the compliance check form

Click "New Check" in the sidebar, or click "Free Pre-Shipment Check" from the landing page. You will see a form with three dropdown fields on a white card.

The compliance check form with three dropdown fields: commodity, origin, and destination

2

Select your commodity ("What are you shipping?")

Click the first dropdown. It contains 154+ commodities, each displayed with its HS code (Harmonised System code) in brackets. For example:

  • • Cocoa Beans [1801.00]
  • • Raw Cashew Nuts [0801.31]
  • • Green Coffee (Arabica) [0901.11]
  • • Shea Butter (refined) [1515.90]
  • • Gold (unwrought) [7108.12]

You can type to search. Start typing the commodity name or HS code and the dropdown filters automatically. For instance, typing "coco" will show Cocoa Beans, Cocoa Butter, Cocoa Paste, and Cocoa Powder.

3

Select the origin country ("Where from?")

Click the second dropdown. It lists 18+ African origin countries, each with a flag emoji:

🇬🇭 Ghana  •  🇨🇮 Côte d'Ivoire  •  🇪🇹 Ethiopia  •  🇰🇪 Kenya  •  🇹🇿 Tanzania  •  🇺🇬 Uganda  •  🇳🇬 Nigeria  •  🇨🇲 Cameroon  •  🇷🇼 Rwanda  •  🇧🇯 Benin  •  🇹🇬 Togo  •  🇲🇬 Madagascar  •  and more

Select the country where the goods are being sourced from. The origin country determines which trade agreements, rules of origin, and regulatory frameworks apply.

4

Select the destination ("Where to?")

Click the third dropdown. There are 7 destination markets available:

3 Understanding Your Results

What the corridor risk rating means, how to read the bar chart, and what regulatory warnings to watch for.

After running a compliance check, you are taken to the results page. This page is divided into several sections. Let us walk through each one.

A. The Corridor Risk Rating

At the top of your results, you will see a large circular score from 0 to 100. This is the Corridor Risk Rating. It measures how complex and risky this particular trade corridor is. A lower score means more regulatory burden and more documents to prepare.

A corridor risk rating of 39 (HIGH RISK) with the four contributing factors shown as a bar chart

The score is calculated from four factors, each shown as a bar in the chart:

FactorWhat It MeasuresMax Penalty
Regulatory RequirementsNumber of active regulatory overlays for this corridor (SPS, EUDR, CBAM, CSDDD, etc.). More overlays = higher penalty.30 points
Product ControlsKnown hazards or special controls for this commodity (e.g. cadmium in cocoa, mycotoxins in nuts, pesticide MRLs).30 points
Document VolumeTotal number of documents you and your supplier will need to prepare. More documents = higher penalty.20 points
Trade RestrictionsSanctions, embargoes, quotas, or special authorisations that apply to this route.20 points

Score interpretation:

70–100: LOW RISK — Straightforward trade with minimal regulatory burden. Few documents needed.

40–69: MODERATE RISK — Several regulatory requirements apply. Prepare documents carefully and allow extra time.

0–39: HIGH RISK — Complex trade corridor with multiple regulatory overlays, known hazards, or restrictions. Start document preparation well in advance.

B. Regulatory warnings

EUDR deforestation regulation warning banner, "Watch this corridor" button, and the Next Actions list

If your commodity triggers specific regulations, you will see coloured warning banners below the score:

  • EUDR (EU Deforestation Regulation) — Yellow banner. Applies to cocoa, coffee, palm oil, soy, rubber, cattle, wood, and their derivatives when shipping to the EU. Click "Prepare EUDR documents" to open the geolocation data form.
  • CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism) — Applies to aluminium, iron, steel, cement, fertiliser, hydrogen, and electricity when shipping to the EU.
  • SPS (Sanitary & Phytosanitary) — Applies to most agricultural products. Requires phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, or fumigation records.
  • CSDDD (Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive) — EU human rights and environmental due diligence requirement for supply chains.

C. Regulatory trigger badges

Below the trade corridor cards, you will see coloured badges showing all possible regulations:

SPS EUDR CBAM — Red badges mean this regulation applies to your corridor.

CITES Conflict Minerals — Grey badges mean this regulation does not apply.

D. Trade corridor detail cards

Three cards display key information about your selected corridor:

  • Commodity card — Shows the product name, HS code, commodity type (agricultural/mineral/manufactured), and which regulatory triggers it activates
  • Origin card — Shows the country name with flag, customs authority, trade bloc membership (ECOWAS, EAC, SADC, etc.), and applicable trade agreements
  • Destination card — Shows the import market, VAT/GST rate, tariff system, and customs authority

E. "Watch this corridor"

Click the "Watch this corridor" button to subscribe to regulatory alerts for this specific trade route. When regulations change, you will be notified. You can watch up to 3 corridors for free. See Section 12: Regulatory Alerts for more details.

4 Next Actions & Document Checklist

Understanding the pending items, your documents vs supplier documents, and how to track status.

Below the corridor risk rating and warnings, you will find the Next Actions section. This is your compliance to-do list — every document and action required to make this shipment compliant.

The document checklist split into YOUR DOCUMENTS (8 items) and SUPPLIER DOCUMENTS (6 items) with Pending status badges

A. How the document list is organised

Documents are split into two groups:

GroupWho is ResponsibleExamples
YOUR DOCUMENTSYou (the importer/buyer)Import licence, customs declaration, insurance certificate, EORI registration, bank confirmation
SUPPLIER DOCUMENTSYour supplier (the exporter)Commercial invoice, bill of lading, certificate of origin, phytosanitary certificate, packing list, fumigation certificate

B. What each item shows

Each line item in the document list displays:

  1. Document name — e.g. "Phytosanitary Certificate", "EUR.1 Movement Certificate"
  2. Description — A brief explanation of what the document is and why it is required
  3. Issuing authority — Who issues this document (e.g. "National Plant Protection Organisation", "Chamber of Commerce")
  4. Timing — When it is needed: Before loading, Before arrival at destination, or Post-arrival
  5. Status badge — The current status of this document

C. Status badges explained

Each document has one of these status badges:

Pending — Document has not been prepared or uploaded yet. This is the default state for all documents when you first run a check.

Ready — Document has been uploaded and verified. It is ready for submission.

Flagged — Document has been reviewed and an issue was found. It needs correction before it can be accepted.

Risk Accepted — You have acknowledged a risk with this document but decided to proceed anyway. This is recorded in the audit trail.

D. Changing document status

You can change the status of any document by clicking on it from the trade detail view. This opens a detail panel where you can:

  1. Mark it as Ready if you have the document prepared
  2. Mark it as Flagged if there is an issue (you will be asked to enter a finding)
  3. Mark it as Risk Accepted if you choose to proceed despite a gap

Every status change is recorded in the trade's event timeline with a timestamp and a cryptographic hash, creating an immutable audit trail.

Tip: The number shown next to "NEXT ACTIONS" (e.g. "14 pending") tells you exactly how many items still need attention. Work through them systematically before generating your TwinLog Trail.

5 Downloading Evidence

TwinLog Trail PDF, Customs data pack CSV, compliance reference numbers, and SHA-256 integrity hashes.

The bottom of the results page showing the "Download TwinLog Trail" button, LC check link, CSV export, and the compliance reference with SHA-256 hash

A. TwinLog Trail PDF

The TwinLog Trail is your audit-ready compliance evidence package. It is a multi-page PDF that captures everything about this trade at the moment it was generated. Click the green "Download TwinLog Trail" button to generate and download it instantly.

The TwinLog Trail PDF contains these pages:

PageContents
1. Cover PageCommodity name, HS code, trade corridor (origin → destination), your company details, TwinLog reference number, generation date
2. TwinLog ScoreCorridor risk rating with the four-factor breakdown (regulatory, product controls, document volume, restrictions) and bar chart
3. Importer RequirementsAll documents and actions required of the importing party (you), with descriptions, issuing authorities, and timing
4. Supplier RequirementsAll documents your supplier must provide, with the same detail level
5. Supplier DocumentsTable of all uploaded documents with filename, upload date, and verification status
6. Regulatory OverlaysActive regulations (SPS, EUDR, CBAM, etc.) and known hazards for this commodity, with detailed descriptions
7. Event TimelineComplete chronological audit trail — every check, upload, verification, flag, and status change with timestamps
8. Audit RecordSHA-256 integrity hash, TwinLog reference, generation timestamp, and hash chain verification data
Important: You must set up your Company Profile (Section 10) before generating a TwinLog Trail. The PDF includes your company name, registered address, VAT number, and EORI number.

B. Customs data pack (CSV)

Click "Customs data pack (CSV)" to download a spreadsheet-compatible file containing all the structured compliance data from your check. This is useful for importing into your own systems, ERP, or for sharing with your customs broker in a machine-readable format.

C. Compliance reference number

Every compliance check generates a unique reference number in the format TT-2026-XXXXXX (e.g. TT-2026-50FF2F). This reference is shown at the bottom of the results page and on every page of the TwinLog Trail PDF.

D. SHA-256 integrity hash

Below the reference number, you will see a long string of characters — this is the SHA-256 integrity hash. It is a cryptographic fingerprint of your compliance data. If any data in the record were changed after generation, the hash would not match. This provides tamper-evident assurance for auditors and regulators.

E. Public verification

Anyone can verify a TwinLog Trail by visiting taptrao.com/verify/TT-2026-XXXXXX (replacing the reference with the actual code). The verification page confirms the record exists, shows the commodity and corridor, and validates the integrity hash.

F. Supplier instructions

Click "Supplier instructions" to generate a pre-formatted message you can send to your supplier. It lists exactly which documents they need to provide, in what format, and by when. You can copy this text and paste it into an email or send it via the built-in supplier request system (see Section 9).

Step 1: Enter LC Terms

Fill in the key terms from your Letter of Credit. These are the values the bank will check against. You will need to enter:

Step 2: Enter Supplier Documents

For each document your supplier has provided, enter the key data fields. The system checks these documents:

  • Commercial Invoice — Invoice number, beneficiary name, goods description, amount, quantity
  • Bill of Lading — B/L number, shipper name, consignee, notify party, ports, shipped-on-board date
  • Certificate of Origin — Issuing authority, origin country, goods description
  • Packing List — Number of packages, net weight, gross weight
  • Insurance Certificate — Coverage amount (must be at least 110% of CIF value per UCP 600), currency
  • Other documents — Phytosanitary certificate, fumigation certificate, quality/weight certificate as required

Step 3: Review

Before submitting, you will see a summary of all the data you have entered for both the LC terms and the supplier documents. Review everything carefully. You can go back to either step to make corrections.

Step 4: Results

After submission, the engine applies UCP 600 (Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits) and ISBP 745 (International Standard Banking Practice) rules to check for discrepancies. Results show one of three verdicts:

COMPLIANT — No discrepancies found. All documents match the LC terms. Safe to present to the bank.

COMPLIANT WITH NOTES — Minor issues detected that may or may not cause rejection. Review the notes and decide whether to correct before presentation.

DISCREPANCIES FOUND — One or more discrepancies detected that are likely to cause bank rejection. Each discrepancy shows:

  1. The UCP 600 article that is violated (e.g. "Article 18(a) — Commercial Invoice")
  2. Which document contains the discrepancy
  3. The LC term value vs the document value
  4. A suggested correction explaining how to fix it

The system also generates a correction email that you can copy and send to your supplier, listing all required corrections in a clear, professional format.

Cost: An LC Document Check uses 1 Shield credit. However, if you are running it as part of a compliance check workflow (from the trade detail page), it is included free with your existing credit. Stand-alone LC checks (without a linked compliance lookup) cost $19.99. Re-checks after corrections cost $9.99 each, with 3 free re-checks included per case.

9 Requesting Supplier Documents

How the Supplier Inbox works: sending secure upload links, tracking uploads, and reviewing received documents.

TapTrao lets you request documents from your supplier without email attachments. Instead, you generate a secure upload link that the supplier can use to upload their documents directly into your trade record.

1

Open your trade and click "Supplier Instructions" or "Request Documents"

From the trade detail page (after running a compliance check), scroll to the bottom and click "Supplier instructions". TapTrao generates a unique, token-gated upload link for this specific trade.

2

Share the upload link with your supplier

Copy the generated link and send it to your supplier via email, WhatsApp, or any other channel. The link looks like: taptrao.com/upload/abc123def456

The supplier does not need a TapTrao account. The link opens a simple, clean upload form.

3

What the supplier sees

When the supplier opens the link, they see a form listing exactly which documents are required for this trade (e.g. Commercial Invoice, Bill of Lading, Certificate of Origin, Phytosanitary Certificate). They upload each file and click submit. They can upload PDF, JPG, or PNG files.

4

Track uploads in your Supplier Inbox

Click "Suppliers" in the sidebar to see all your supplier requests and their status. Each request shows:

  • The trade corridor and commodity
  • Whether the supplier has opened the link
  • Which documents have been uploaded so far
  • Upload timestamps
5

Review uploaded documents

Once the supplier uploads documents, they appear in the trade's document list. You can then verify or flag each document as described in Section 4.

Security note: Supplier upload links are unique tokens. They cannot be guessed and expire after use. The supplier can only upload documents for the specific trade they were invited to — they cannot see your compliance results, TwinLog Score, or any other data.

After running a compliance check, click "Save as Template" on the results page. The template stores the commodity, origin, and destination selections along with a snapshot of the compliance data at that point in time.

Stale detection

Templates include a built-in freshness check. When you view your templates, TapTrao compares the saved snapshot data against current regulatory data. If anything has changed, the template is marked with an Stale badge and the age (e.g. "45 days old").

This tells you that regulations may have changed since you last checked this corridor and you should re-run the compliance check before relying on the old data.

Using a template

  1. Go to "Templates" in the sidebar
  2. Find the template you want and click "Use Template"
  3. This opens the compliance check form with all three fields pre-selected
  4. Click "Check compliance risk" to run a fresh check using this corridor

Refreshing a stale template

If a template is marked as stale, click "Refresh". TapTrao re-runs the compliance check for that corridor and updates the template's snapshot with the latest regulatory data. This uses 1 Shield credit.

Deleting a template

Click "Delete" to remove a template you no longer need. This action is permanent.

14 Demurrage Calculator

Estimate port demurrage costs when container delays occur.

Demurrage is the charge levied by shipping lines when containers are not picked up from the port within the agreed free period. Document delays — missing phytosanitary certificates, incomplete customs declarations, or LC discrepancies — are a common cause of demurrage. The Demurrage Calculator helps you estimate the financial impact.

Go to "Demurrage Calc" in the sidebar under the Compliance section to access the calculator.

The calculator uses an escalating tier model that reflects how most shipping lines structure their demurrage charges:
